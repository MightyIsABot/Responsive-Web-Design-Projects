Nikola Tesla: The Genius Who Lit the World

Biography

  • Born: July 10, 1856, Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • Died: January 7, 1943, New York City, USA
  • Education: Graz University of Technology, but never completed a degree.
  • Background: Serbian-American inventor, engineer, and futurist. Moved to the U.S. in 1884 to work for Thmoas Edison.

Major Contributions

  • Alternating Current (AC): Developed the AC power system, which became the standard over Edisonâ€™s Direct Current (DC) due to its efficiency over long distances.
  • Tesla Coil: Created a high-voltage transformer circuit still used in radio technology today.
  • Radio & Wireless Energy: Worked on wireless transmission before Marconi. The Supreme Court later credited Tesla as the true inventor of radio.
  • Induction Motor: Developed the motor that powered industries and revolutionized machinery.
  • Wireless Power (Wardenclyffe Tower):Attempted to create a system for wireless global energy transmission.

Challenges

  • Lacked financial support.
  • Died poor despite his groundbreaking contributions.
  • Often overshadowed by rivals like Thomas Edison.

Legacy

  • The Tesla Unit for magnetic field strength is named after him.
  • Tesla Motors was named in his honor.
  • His work laid the foundation for modern electricity, radio, and wireless communication.

Famous Quote

"The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."
